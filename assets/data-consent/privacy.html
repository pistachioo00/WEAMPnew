Background

Republic Act No. 10173, also known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA), aims to protect personal data in information and communications systems both in the government and the private sector. It ensures that entities or organizations processing personal data establish policies, and implement measures and procedures that guarantee the safety and security of personal data under their control or custody, thereby upholding an individual’s data privacy rights. A personal information controller or personal information processor is instructed to implement reasonable and appropriate measures to protect personal data against natural dangers such as accidental loss or destruction, and human dangers such as unlawful access, fraudulent misuse, unlawful destruction, alteration and contamination.

To inform its personnel of such measures, each personal information controller or personal information processor is expected to produce a Privacy Manual. The Manual serves as a guide or handbook for ensuring the compliance of an organization or entity with the DPA, its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), and other relevant issuances of the National Privacy Commission (NPC). It also encapsulates the privacy and data protection protocols that need to be observed and carried out within the organization for specific circumstances (e.g., from collection to destruction), directed toward the fulfillment and realization of the rights of data subject.

Introduction

This Data Privacy Manual is a result of the Worker’s Affairs Office (WAO) to comply with Republic Act No. 10173 or the Data Privacy Act of 2012, its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), and other related policies, including other NPC issuances. This may be reviewed and updated by the WAO based on the experiences and initial results of the ongoing implementation.

This Manual provides the basis for the Office and its employees to operate data security and protection measures and to integrate data security measures in the performance of their functions.

The law requires the office and its employees to understand and apply basic data security principles of the Data Privacy Act, listed as follows:

  • Transparency. The nature, extent, and purpose of data collection are made clear to all data subjects by WAO. Furthermore, the office must inform data subjects about the processing of their data and its implications on their right to privacy.
  • Legitimate Purpose. WAO is responsible for ensuring that all personal data processing has a legal basis or a legitimate purpose. The Data Privacy Act requires the office to make sure that data processing complies with the stated purpose of its programs.
  • Proportionality. The WAO should limit its personal data collection, storage, processing, and usage to what is required to fulfill its program requirements or purposes.

The office ensures that through this Manual all personal data collected from all its relevant data subjects and beneficiaries shall be processed in adherence to the general principles of transparency, legitimate purpose, and proportionality.

Definition of Terms

For purposes of this Manual the following terms are defined as follows:

  • Conciliation-Mediation refers to the objective to resolve disputes arising from employment or labor-related matters.
  • Consent of Data Subject refers to any freely expressed, explicit, and informed indication of will that a data subject gives to permit the gathering and processing of personal data about and/or relating to him or her. Consent shall be attested by a written, electronic or recorded means. It may also be given on behalf of the data subject by an agent specifically authorized by the data subject to do so.
  • Data Subject refers to an individual whose personal, sensitive personal or privileged information is processed by WAO. This term shall apply to the personal data of its beneficiaries.
  • Manual refers to the Data Privacy Manual of the Worker’s Affairs Office.
  • Personal Information refers to any information, including but not limited to name, address, email address, and mobile number, whether or not it is recorded in a material form and from which the identity of a person is evident or can be reasonably and directly determined by the entity holding the information, or which, when combined with other information, would absolutely and directly identify a person.
  • Personal Information Controller (PIC) refers to a natural or juridical person, or any other body who controls the processing of personal data, or instructs another to process personal data on his behalf.
  • Personal Information Processor (PIP) refers to any natural or juridical person or any other body to whom a PIC may outsource or instruct the processing of personal data pertaining to a data subject.
  • "Processing" refers to any action taken upon personal information which may include the collection, recording, organization, storage, updating or modification, retrieval, consultation, use, consolidation, blocking, erasure or destruction of data.
  • Request for Assistance (RFA) refers to the formal request for SENA's involvement in facilitating a resolution between the both parties through a conciliation-mediation process.
  • Requesting Party refers to any party, such as an employer, worker (including the domestic workers), group of workers (local or even overseas), union, worker association, or federation, who files a Request for Assistance (RFA).
  • Responding Party refers to any person or entity, including individuals and organizations, who are invited to participate in a conciliation-mediation process conducted by SENA.
  • Sensitive Personal Information refers to personal information:
    • Data relating to an individual's race, ethnic origin, marital status, age, color, and religious, philosophical or political affiliations;
    • Data relating to an individual's health education, genetic or sexual life of a person, or to any proceeding for any offense committed or alleged to have been committed by such individual, the disposal of such proceeding, or the sentence of any court in such proceedings;
    • Data relating to any issued by government agencies peculiar to an individual which includes, but is not limited to, social security numbers, previous or current health records, licenses or its denials, suspension or revocation, and tax returns.
  • Single Entry Approach (SENA) refers to the government program that helps employers and workers settle their workplace issues quickly, fairly, and cheaply through a 30-day mediation process.
  • SENA Desk Officer (SEADO) refers to the trained personnel in the DOLE or its attached agencies who provide interview, advice, and assistance, and conduct conciliation-mediation services.
  • Withdrawal refers to a written statement from the requesting party, expressing their decision to terminate the conciliation-mediation or withdraw their RFA.
  • Workers Affairs Office (WAO) means a government office within the jurisdiction of Local Government of Valenzuela or under the City Mayor’s Office that promotes the enlightenment of workers concerning their rights and obligations through a conciliation-mediation.

Scope and Limitations

This Manual shall apply solely to the Valenzuela City Worker’s Affairs Office. It shall be strictly followed by all its distinguished employees under the institution, regardless of position and employment status, including third-party software developers, contractors, and other service providers that may have direct or indirect access to databases and electronic storage that contains personal data of its beneficiaries and of the body.

This Manual serves as the main manual of the WAO and shall be a guide for its Single Entry Approach (SENA) and its filing of Request For Assistance (RFA).

Processing of Personal Data

This section outlines the various phases of data processing within Workers Affairs Office:

Collection

The collection of both personal information and sensitive personal information must be collected legally, for a legitimate reason and is directly related and necessary in achieving the objective of WAO and fulfillment of its function in accordance with the charter.

Personal information of data subjects are obtained in a transparent manner through the filling up of official forms either physically or electronically. These forms are essential in the provision of service to requesting parties in relation to avail RFA in its totality.

Use

The use of personal data shall only be allowed when it is necessary to the goals and objectives of WAO. Use of personal data may also be allowed if:

  • To protect the vital interests (welfare and labor related concern) of an individual;
  • For compliance with a legal obligation under DOLE which the office is required;
  • In compliance with other laws and regulations and other legal purposes;
  • For consent given by the data subject or authorized representative; and
  • To pursue the legal process as required in addressing the Request for Assistance (RFA)

Storage

Personal data collected shall be stored in a manner that is secure and protected against unauthorized access, loss, destruction or any form of misuse. Records should be stored with security measures that are in compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

Retention

The WAO shall retain personal data for as long as necessary to achieve the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by applicable laws and regulations. Data retention periods are aligned with legal requirements and organizational policies.

Disposal

When personal data is no longer required or is deemed obsolete, it shall be disposed of in a secure manner to ensure that it cannot be reconstructed or retrieved. Disposal methods include secure deletion of electronic files and proper shredding of physical documents.

Access Control

Access to personal data shall be limited to authorized personnel only. Security measures, such as access controls and authentication protocols, are implemented to prevent unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure of personal data.

Data Sharing

Data sharing with third parties is conducted in compliance with legal and regulatory requirements. Agreements are established with third parties to ensure that personal data is handled with the same level of protection as required by WAO’s policies.

Training and Awareness

WAO provides training and awareness programs to its personnel to ensure that they are informed of their responsibilities regarding data privacy and protection. Regular updates and refreshers are provided to maintain compliance with relevant laws and policies.

Privacy Rights of Data Subjects

The following rights are afforded to data subjects under the Data Privacy Act of 2012:

  • Right to be Informed – The right to be informed of the collection and processing of personal data.
  • Right to Access – The right to access personal data held by WAO.
  • Right to Object – The right to object to the processing of personal data.
  • Right to Erasure or Blocking – The right to request the erasure or blocking of personal data that is inaccurate or processed unlawfully.
  • Right to Data Portability – The right to request that personal data be transferred to another data controller.
  • Right to Rectification – The right to request the correction of inaccurate personal data.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint – The right to file a complaint with the National Privacy Commission if rights are violated.

Compliance and Enforcement

Compliance with this Manual is mandatory for all employees, and adherence to data privacy regulations will be monitored regularly. Violations of the Manual or data privacy laws may result in disciplinary actions, including termination of employment and legal consequences.

The Data Privacy Manual will be reviewed periodically and updated as necessary to ensure continued compliance with applicable laws and regulations.
